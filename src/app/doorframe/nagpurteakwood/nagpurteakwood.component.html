Origin

Nagpur Teak, often referred to as Central Province Teak (CP Teak), originates from the forests in and around the Nagpur region in the Indian state of Maharashtra. This region is known for its dense tropical forests, which provide the ideal growing conditions for teak trees.

Advantages

1.Exceptional Durability

Nagpur teak is extremely durable, withstanding wear and tear over long periods. It’s known to last for decades

2. High Oil Content

Similar to other types of teak, Nagpur teak has a high natural oil content. This oil helps protect the wood from moisture, rot, and fungal decay, making it highly resistant to the elements

3. Resistance to Termites and Pests

Nagpur teak is highly resistant to termites, wood borers, and other pests. The natural oils act as a deterrent to insects, reducing the need for chemical treatments

4. Dimensional Stability

The wood is less prone to warping, cracking, or shrinking, even when exposed to changing weather conditions. This makes it an excellent choice for use in humid or varying climates

5. Aesthetic Appeal

Nagpur teak has a rich golden-brown color with an attractive straight grain. The wood darkens gracefully over time, acquiring a deeper, more elegant appearance, which enhances its beauty

6. Low Maintenance

Due to its high oil content and resistance to decay, Nagpur teak requires minimal maintenance. Occasional oiling or polishing helps retain its luster, but it doesn't demand frequent care
