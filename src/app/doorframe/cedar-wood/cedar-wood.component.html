Origin

Deodar forests are forests dominated by Cedrus deodara, the deodar cedar. This tree is found naturally in the Western Himalayas from the Gandaki River in central Nepal to the Hindu Kush mountain range in Afghanistan.

Advantages

1)Cedar thrives in damp climates, enabling it to withstand many conditions Cedar doesn’t shrink, swell, warp, or decay even when there are severe changes in weather, which makes it such an ideal choice for exterior siding

2)Cedar is a porous wood, which gives it the ability to absorb noise This quality is one of the many reasons why it’s commonly applied to the exterior and interior of homes and buildings

3)The oil in cedar wood can control pests like termites, ants, moths, mosquitoes, and cockroaches

4)As described above, white and red cedar have natural color combinations, so we typically just use a clear coat to magnify its natural color Finishing can enhance the durability of your cedar wood even more with regular applications of sealer to help it retain its color and stability
